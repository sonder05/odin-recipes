Kaymak

About

Adjaran khachapuri is a traditional Georgian bread known for its distinctive boat shape and rich, savory filling. The dough is baked until golden, then filled with a blend of melted cheeses that bubble and brown in the oven. Just before serving, a raw egg and a pat of butter are placed in the center, where the residual heat gently cooks the egg, creating a creamy, indulgent dish meant to be mixed and eaten hot.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 3/4 cup warm water or milk
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta
  • 2-3 eggs (one per khachapuri)
  • Butter, for topping

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix warm water (or milk), sugar, and yeast, and let it sit for a few minutes until foamy.
  2. Add flour, salt, and olive oil, then knead until a soft, elastic dough forms. Cover and let it rise for about 1 hour, until doubled in size.
  3. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C). Divide the dough into portions and roll each into an oval.
  4. Roll the edges inward on both sides and pinch the ends to form a boat shape.
  5. Mix the cheeses together and fill the center of each dough boat.
  6. Bake for 12–15 minutes, until the bread is golden and the cheese is melted.
  7. Remove from the oven, add an egg to the center of each khachapuri, and return to the oven for 2–3 minutes until the egg white is just set.
  8. Top with a small piece of butter and serve hot, mixing the egg, butter, and cheese before eating.
